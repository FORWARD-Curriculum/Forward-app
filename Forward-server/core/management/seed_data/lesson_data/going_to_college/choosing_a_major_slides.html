Lots of people start college with one major and decide to change later. You can always change your major! Try to get a good feel for it early by taking classes in the subject. Completing your degree may take longer if you change your major repeatedly or after too long.

Take introductory classes in several subjects that you think you might be interested in. Introductory classes are a great way to try out different classes and can help you get to know faculty and students.

Talk to faculty and students from departments you are interested in. Sometimes departments hold workshops and socials you can attend, and interacting with other students and faculty can help you figure out if the department is the right fit for you.

Learn about careers that are available with a major. Different majors lead to different career opportunities. If you have a specific career in mind, you’ll want a major that works toward that career. If that career falls through, are you open to the other career opportunities?

Consider the availability of careers with a given major. Are there lots of jobs available or will you have to compete for the job you want? Is the field growing or becoming more limited? For example, while poetry might be a hobby with lots to learn, the jobs related to poetry are often very limited.

Classes in a particular subject can get harder as you progress in your degree. You will want to consider what academic strengths you have and see if you can align your major with those strengths.

Different majors and programs may have different requirements for completing your degree, such as completing an internship or thesis. Learn about these requirements before committing to a major and determine if they are right for you.

To get the career you want, what level of education will you need? Some careers may require a higher level of education. Before committing to a major, it’s wise to have a good idea of all that it might require.
